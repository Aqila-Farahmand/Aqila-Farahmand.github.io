What stays with you when you leave a country behind? Is it the syntax of a new language, the taste of the local air, or the way the people define 'happiness'? Having called Afghanistan, India, Italy, Norway, and recently Luxembourg home, I have learned that culture isn't something you observe, it is something you absorb. My journey has been a masterclass in adaptation, and it all began with a life changing chapter in India.

My Journey to India

Fresh out of high school, I swapped the familiar comforts of home for the rigorous halls of NIT Kurukshetra. It was a leap of faith: my first time living away from my family and my first time navigating a world entirely in English, having studied in Farsi all my life. The Indian education system was very different from the one I was used to, but the real classroom was outside the lecture hall. I found my rhythm on the volleyball court. Playing for the university team wasn't just about winning the 2018 ITUSA (Inter-Technology University Sports Association) championship; it was where I learned Hindi, the key that truly unlocked India for me. From the sun-soaked beaches of Goa to the mountain air of Shimla, I traveled far and wide, but nothing compared to Kashmir. Thanks to a university friends incredible family, I experienced the legendary hospitality of Srinagar. I can still see the reflection of the mountains on Dal Lake from Char Chinar Island and remember the horse riding through the valley of Pahalgam as we trekked through the 'Mini Switzerland' of India.
